# COMPUTATION METHODOLOGY

## Section 1: Power Calculation

### Original Content:
The power produced by solar panels can be calculated using the formula:  
**P = V * I**  
Where:
- P is power (in watts)
- V is voltage (in volts)
- I is current (in amperes)

### Layman's Explanation:
This equation helps us understand how much electrical power the solar panel generates. By multiplying the voltage (the electrical force) with the current (the flow of electric charge), we can find the total power output of the panel.

### Real-World Example:
If a solar panel outputs 20 volts and 5 amperes, then the power output is:
P = 20V * 5A = 100 watts. This means in ideal conditions, the solar panel can supply 100 watts of power.


## Section 2: Energy Storage Calculation

### Original Content:
Energy stored in batteries can be calculated using the formula:  
**E = P * t**  
Where:
- E is energy (in watt-hours)
- P is power (in watts)
- t is time (in hours)

### Layman's Explanation:
This formula gives us the total energy the batteries can hold over a period. By multiplying the power output of the solar panels with how long they produce that power, we calculate the total energy stored.

### Real-World Example:
If a solar panel produces 100 watts for 5 hours, then the energy stored will be:
E = 100W * 5h = 500 watt-hours. This means the batteries can store enough power to run a 500-watt device for one hour.


## Section 3: Efficiency Calculation

### Original Content:
The efficiency of the solar system can be calculated as:  
**Efficiency = (P_out / P_in) * 100%**  
Where:
- P_out is output power
- P_in is input power

### Layman's Explanation:
Efficiency tells us how well the solar system converts sunlight into usable power. The higher the efficiency percentage, the better the system performs.

### Real-World Example:
If the input power is 500 watts (from sunlight) and the output power is 400 watts (to the devices), then:
Efficiency = (400W / 500W) * 100% = 80%. This means 80% of the sunlight energy is converted into usable electrical energy.
